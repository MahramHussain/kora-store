Computing Group Project 
Case study for Assessment 1 
 
For the Computing Group Project module, you are required to work in groups for 
Assessment 1. 
Case Study: Online Retail System for an Independent Business 
Your group has been approached by a hypothetical independent retail business that wishes 
to establish a dedicated online sales system for its products. 
The business already sells products through informal digital channels (e.g. social media or 
messaging apps) and now requires a more structured, scalable, and inclusive web-based 
system to support online sales and customer management. 
The company has no predefined technical solution and expects your team to analyse its 
needs and propose an appropriate design and implementation. 
Your Task 
Your group must design and implement a web-based retail system that meets the needs of 
this business. 
1. Define the business and users 
- Decide what products the business sells. 
- Identify and describe the target users of the system. 
- Justify your choices and explain how they influenced your requirements and design 
decisions. 
2. Plan and manage the project 
- Plan how your group will design and develop the system using appropriate project 
management methods and tools. 
- Allocate and justify team roles and responsibilities. 
- Provide evidence of project planning and coordination (e.g. Gantt chart, backlog, risk 
analysis, meeting records). 
3. Design and implement the system 
- Implement the system using custom development or an appropriate e-commerce or 
prototyping platform (e.g. Figma, Wix, Shopify). 
- Your choice of tools and technologies must be justified. 
Core Functional Requirements 
Your system must support the following minimum features: 
- A product catalogue with a minimum of 20 products (guideline). 
- Each product must include an image, description, and price. 
- Users must be able to browse products, add products to a shopping cart, update quantities, 
and remove items. An AI “search mode” is welcome but not compulsory.  
- User account creation and login functionality. 
- Checkout process including order review and entry of delivery or contact details. 
 
Design Quality and Enhancement 
To achieve higher marks, your group is expected to go beyond basic functionality by 
demonstrating: 
- Clear and justified information architecture. 
- Meaningful filtering or sorting of products. 
- Thoughtful user experience design. 
- Consideration of Equality, Diversity and Inclusion (EDI) as part of system design. 
Optional enhancements may include order tracking, promotional features, customer 
support features, or basic admin functionality. 
 
Critical Analysis and Reflection 
Your report should demonstrate that your group has: 
- Analysed existing online retail systems. 
- Critically evaluated design features. 
- Made conscious design decisions. 
- Reflected on teamwork, planning, and EDI considerations.
